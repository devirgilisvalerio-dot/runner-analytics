Acute:Chronic Workload Ratio (ACWR)

ACWR compares your last 7 days of load (km) against your 28-day average. 0.8–1.3 = safe training zone. > 1.3 = overreaching, elevated injury risk. > 1.5 = high injury risk. < 0.8 = undertraining / deload week.

Pace Distribution

How much time you spend at each pace. Bars to the left mean fast running; bars to the right mean easy or recovery effort. A well-trained runner typically shows a broad aerobic base (5:00–6:00) with a sharp spike at race pace.

Running Efficiency (Power vs Pace)

Each dot is one run. X-axis = average power output (watts); Y-axis = average pace. Dots moving toward the bottom-left over time mean you're running faster with less effort — the key sign of improving fitness.
